In the middle of the 21st century, almost everyone is co-located. We live everywhere and subsequently, we work everywhere. While technology has improved, we still need to it communicate with all the people on our teams. Just look at the consortium: there are 12 members and only two of them live in the same country as each other. This really started in your time. Already, a lot of people at SAP work from home in one of the dozens of countries where SAP has a presence.

The point of all this is that being able to communicate effectively is important. One of the primary ways that you need to learn how to interact with people at SAP is by using Lync or Skype for Business. To help you get up to speed, you should check out this course.
