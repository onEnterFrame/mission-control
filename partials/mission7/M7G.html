How do we go about solving a problem? I found a course for you here that will describe a five-step process for problem-solving.

Before we get into it, I want to re-iterate how important it is to identify the consequences when describing the problem. Maybe if we followed this from the gecko,we had been applied these problem-solving steps, we wouldn't be in this environmental disasttaster in the first place!

The consequences are the effects of the problem such as impact to customers, profit, productivity or morale (in the context of the workplace). Identifying the consequences involves asking a series of questions such as:

  1. How much is the problem costing?
  2. Are customers affected? To what degree?
  3. Does the problem exist in other processes?
  4. Is it creating problems in other processes? What are they?
  5. How many other people are affected by the problem?
  6. Are there other potential consequences?
